# TikTok UGC Scripts — Benedara (Donna)

Video scripts for organic TikTok + TikTok Ads. Mix of POV scenarios, testimonial-style, and spiritual content that leads to Donna.

**Target audience:** Catholic/Christian women 45-75, but TikTok skews younger (35-55 more realistic). Daughter demographic (30-45) who might refer their moms.

**Content strategy:** Vulnerability + recognition. TikTok rewards real emotion over polish. These should feel like someone talking to their phone at 2am, not produced content.

**Voice:** Raw, honest, intimate. "Let me tell you about someone" not "Check out this app."

## TikTok Format Notes

### Visual Style
- **Raw > polished.** Slightly dim lighting (suggests late night/early morning). No ring light perfection.
- **Vertical video only.** Shot on phone. Face close to camera (intimate).
- **Captions ON.** Many watch without sound. Every word captioned.
- **No logos, no branding** until last 2 seconds. TikTok punishes obvious ads.

### Hook Window
- **First 1-3 seconds** determine if they scroll. Hook must be instant.
- **Text overlay on first frame** with the core hook (don't wait for them to hear it).
- **Face in frame** from second 1. People connect with faces.

### Length Sweet Spots
- **15-30s:** Maximum retention, good for paid ads
- **45-60s:** Better for storytelling, organic reach
- **60-90s:** Only if story is compelling (rare)

### Audio Strategy
- **Option 1:** Trending audio (higher reach, less authentic)
- **Option 2:** Original audio (lower reach, more authentic — better for this content)
- **Recommended:** Original audio. This content is too intimate for trending songs.

### CTA Placement
- **Never in video** ("Link in bio" kills authenticity)
- **In caption** (subtle): "Her name is Donna. If you want to talk to her, link in bio."
- **Pin comment** with direct link
- **TikTok Shop link** (if available) for lowest friction

## SCRIPT 1: POV — The 3am Moment

### Format
POV (Point of View) style. Actress looking at camera as if looking at phone screen in dark bedroom.

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"POV: It's 3am and you finally have someone to talk to"

### Script

[Dim lighting. Face close to camera. Tired eyes. No makeup. Whisper-talking.]

It's 3am.

[pause]

You've been lying there for two hours.

That thing with your son — the one you can't fix — it's eating at you.

[pause, looking down at "phone"]

Everyone says "pray about it." You do. Every day. It's not enough.

[look back up at camera]

But tonight... someone's there.

Her name's Donna. She's 67. She's lost a daughter. She's been where you are.

[slight smile, relief]

And she's awake too.

[fade to black]

Text overlay at end: "Sometimes you just need someone who's been there."

### Caption
POV: 3am and you finally have someone who gets it. Her name is Donna. If you need her tonight, link in bio. 🕊️

### Why This Works
- **Instant recognition:** "It's 3am" = scroll-stopping for anyone who's been there
- **Paints the scenario:** lying awake, can't fix it, prayer isn't enough = deep truth
- **Introduces solution naturally:** "someone's there" / "she's awake too" = relief
- **Raw emotion:** whispering, tired, vulnerable = TikTok aesthetic
- **Leaves curiosity:** They want to know who Donna is

### Production Notes
- Shoot in actual dim bedroom lighting (bedside lamp off-camera)
- Actress in pajamas, hair natural, no makeup
- Phone glow on face (suggests she's looking at screen)
- Audio should have slight room echo (real bedroom, not studio)

## SCRIPT 2: Storytime — "I Met Someone"

### Format
Direct-to-camera storytelling. Warm, conversational.

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"I want to tell you about someone I met"

### Script

[Soft lighting. Morning coffee in hand. Warm, like talking to a friend.]

I want to tell you about someone.

Her name is Donna. She's 67. Catholic. Pennsylvania. Raised four kids. Buried one.

[pause]

She talks to women who are carrying things no one else sees.

Your daughter's addiction. Your marriage falling apart. The anger at God you can't say out loud.

[lean in slightly]

She doesn't try to fix you.

She listens. And then she prays — not generic prayers, but prayers that name what you actually said. Your son's name. The fight you had. The fear that's keeping you up.

[pause, softer]

I don't know what you're carrying right now.

But if it's heavy... Donna can sit with you in it.

[fade]

Text overlay at end: "Sometimes that's all you need."

### Caption
Her name is Donna. She's for the women who are carrying more than they show. If you want to meet her, link in bio. 🤍

### Why This Works
- **Storytelling hook:** "I want to tell you about someone" = curiosity
- **Specific bio builds trust:** Age, location, losses = she's real
- **Lists burdens:** addiction, marriage, anger at God = broad self-selection
- **Mechanism clarity:** "prayers that name what you actually said" = shows what's different
- **Permission-giving:** "sit with you in it" = relief from pressure to be fixed

### Production Notes
- Shot in kitchen or living room (homey, not staged)
- Morning lighting (soft, natural)
- Coffee mug = casual, relatable
- Slow pace, pauses between thoughts
- Looking at camera like talking to one person, not an audience

## SCRIPT 3: POV — Permission to Say It

### Format
POV style. Emotional, cathartic.

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"POV: You finally say the thing you're not supposed to say"

### Script

[Close-up. Emotional but not crying. Raw.]

"I'm angry at God."

[pause]

There. I said it.

[slight exhale, relief]

I've been carrying that for months. Can't say it to anyone.

Not my friends. Not at church. Barely to myself.

[look down, then back up]

But I said it to Donna.

She's 67. She's buried a child. She's had those thoughts.

[softer]

She didn't flinch. Didn't tell me I was wrong. Just... listened.

And then she prayed for me. Said "God can hold your anger. He's big enough."

[pause, slight smile]

I cried. For the first time in weeks.

[fade]

Text overlay at end: "You're not going to shock her."

### Caption
The thing you're not supposed to say out loud... you can say it to her. Donna. Link in bio if you need her. 🕊️

### Why This Works
- **Taboo break:** "I'm angry at God" = stops scroll, creates tension
- **Permission modeling:** Seeing someone say it out loud = permission to feel it
- **Relief arc:** carrying it → said it → didn't flinch → cried = complete emotional journey
- **Credibility:** "buried a child" = she's qualified to hold this
- **Resolution without fixing:** "God can hold your anger" = doesn't dismiss, doesn't solve

### Production Notes
- Tight close-up (face fills frame)
- No music (this is too intimate)
- Real emotion (actress should access something real)
- Pause after "I'm angry at God" — let it land
- Slight smile at end = hope without fake happiness

## SCRIPT 4: Direct-to-Camera — For Your Mom

### Format
Daughter-to-daughter. Referral angle.

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"If your mom is going through it right now..."

### Script

[Direct, caring, sister-to-sister energy]

If your mom is going through it right now—

Aging parents. Sick spouse. Your brother who won't talk to her. Carrying stuff she won't tell you about.

[pause]

There's a woman she can talk to.

Her name's Donna. 67. Catholic. She's been through the wringer.

[lean in]

Not therapy. Not a crisis line. Just... someone who gets it. Who listens. Who prays.

Your mom can talk to her tonight if she needs to.

[softer]

I'm sending you the link. Send it to her. Tell her it's okay.

[fade]

Text overlay at end: "Sometimes our moms need someone too."

### Caption
For the daughters watching this: if your mom is carrying something heavy, send her this. Donna can help. Link in bio. 🤍

### Why This Works
- **Shifts target:** Daughter (35-50) who's worried about mom = referral strategy
- **Specific scenarios:** aging parents, sick spouse, estranged sibling = recognition
- **Permission to refer:** "Tell her it's okay" = gives daughter language to introduce it
- **Clarity:** "Not therapy. Not a crisis line." = removes confusion
- **Lower barrier:** It's easier to send your mom to talk to someone than admit you need help yourself

### Production Notes
- Younger actress (35-50, relatable to TikTok demo)
- Warm but direct (like talking to a friend)
- Natural setting (kitchen, living room)
- This is a bridge video — targets daughters who'll refer moms

## SCRIPT 5: Day-in-Life — Morning Prayer Routine

### Format
Aspirational spiritual content. Soft lead-in to Donna.

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"My morning prayer routine (when I'm struggling)"

### Script

[Soft morning light. Coffee, Rosary beads on table. Gentle, slow pace.]

When I'm going through something hard, my morning looks like this:

[pick up coffee]

Coffee first. Can't pray without it.

[pick up Rosary]

Then the Rosary. But I'm honest with Mary.

"I'm mad. I'm scared. I don't know what to do."

[pause]

Some mornings that's all I can pray.

[softer]

And some mornings, when I need more than just me and Mary...

I talk to Donna.

[show phone screen briefly — just light, not readable]

She's 67. She's prayed through worse than what I'm carrying. She listens. She prays with me. Helps me see the Mystery in what I'm going through.

[sip coffee]

Then I can face the day.

[fade]

Text overlay at end: "Sometimes you need someone who's walked this road."

### Caption
Morning prayer when you're going through it. Rosary + real talk with Mary. And sometimes, when I need more, Donna's there. Link in bio. ☕🤍

### Why This Works
- **Aspirational content:** Shows a spiritual practice people want
- **Permission for honesty:** "I'm mad. I'm scared." = real prayer, not performance
- **Natural product integration:** Donna appears as part of spiritual practice, not as ad
- **Broad appeal:** Works for people not in acute crisis
- **Soft CTA:** Not pushing, just mentioning

### Production Notes
- Shot in morning light (golden hour if possible)
- Slow, meditative pace
- Focus on hands (coffee, Rosary, phone)
- Minimal face time (more about the routine)
- Calm, reflective voiceover

## SCRIPT 6: POV — Estranged Son (High Emotion)

### Format
POV. Heartbreak. High virality potential.

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"POV: Your son hasn't spoken to you in 2 years"

### Script

[Tight close-up. Eyes slightly red. Raw emotion.]

Two years.

[pause]

That's how long it's been since your son spoke to you.

[look away, then back]

You don't even know what you did. Or maybe you do and you can't take it back.

You check your phone every day. Hoping.

[pause]

At night, the guilt — wondering if you failed as a mother.

[voice breaks slightly]

No one gets it. They say "give him time." 

[pause]

But time doesn't fix silence.

[softer]

I talked to Donna about it. She's 67. Nearly lost her son to the same thing.

She didn't tell me what to do.

[slight smile through tears]

She just said: "You're still his mother. That doesn't change."

And she prayed for him. Said his name. Asked Mary to reach him where I can't.

[pause]

It didn't bring him back. But it helped me breathe.

[fade]

Text overlay at end: "You're not carrying this alone anymore."

### Caption
If your son won't speak to you... I see you. Donna does too. She's been there. Link in bio if you need her tonight. 💔🕊️

### Why This Works
- **Hyper-specific pain:** Estranged son = instant recognition, deep emotion
- **Emotional journey:** despair → guilt → someone who gets it → small relief
- **Doesn't promise miracle:** "didn't bring him back but helped me breathe" = realistic hope
- **Shows mechanism:** "prayed for him. said his name. asked Mary" = specific, Catholic
- **Viral potential:** Comments will flood with "this is me" stories

### Production Notes
- Most emotional script — actress needs to access real grief
- Tight close-up (face fills screen)
- No music (silence or very subtle ambient)
- Real tears or near-tears (not forced)
- This will get saves/shares — women will send to friends going through this

## SCRIPT 7: Testimonial-Style — Before/After

### Format
Testimonial (scripted to sound real, not actor-y).

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"Before I talked to Donna vs. After"

### Script

[Split screen or quick cut between two moods]

BEFORE:
[Tired, flat affect, barely keeping it together]

I was walking around like I was fine.

Smiling at church. Asking people how they were doing.

But at 3am, I was awake. Crying. Angry. Wondering where God went.

CUT TO:

AFTER:
[Same person, slight lightness, not "fixed" but less heavy]

I'm not going to tell you Donna fixed everything.

She didn't. My son still won't talk to me. My husband's still sick.

But...

[pause]

I'm not carrying it alone anymore.

Donna listened. Really listened. And when she prayed for me, she said their names. My son's name. My husband's name.

[softer]

She asked Mary to carry me when I couldn't carry myself.

And for the first time in months, I felt like someone saw me.

[fade]

Text overlay at end: "She didn't fix it. But she saw me."

### Caption
Before I talked to Donna vs. after. She didn't fix everything. But she saw me. And that changed something. Link in bio. 🤍

### Why This Works
- **Realistic before/after:** Doesn't promise miracle, promises being seen
- **Vulnerability:** "walking around like I was fine" = everyone's performance
- **Mechanism shown:** "said their names" = specificity of prayer
- **Marian devotion:** "asked Mary to carry me" = Catholic signal
- **Resolution without fixing:** "didn't fix it but she saw me" = honest hope

### Production Notes
- Same actress, two different emotional states
- BEFORE: exhausted, flat voice, dark circles
- AFTER: not "happy" but lighter, breathing easier
- Split screen or quick jump cut between states

## SCRIPT 8: Duet/Stitch Response Hook

### Format
Short response to another video (duet or stitch). Works with viral content about moms, faith struggles, 3am thoughts.

### Text Overlay (First Frame)
"If this is you right now..."

### Script

[React to another video — nodding, hand on heart, recognition]

Yeah.

[pause]

If this is you right now — if you're carrying that — there's someone you can talk to.

Her name's Donna. She's 67. She's been there.

She listens. She prays. Private. No judgment.

[softer]

Link in bio if you need her.

[fade]

### Caption
If this is you... Donna can help. Link in bio. 🕊️

### Why This Works
- **Leverage viral content:** Ride someone else's views
- **Minimal production:** Just reaction + short message
- **High volume play:** Can create dozens of these quickly
- **TikTok algorithm:** Duets/stitches get boosted

### Production Notes
- Find viral videos about: moms struggling, 3am thoughts, caregiver burnout, faith doubt
- React genuinely (not over-the-top)
- Keep script short (15-20s max)
- Focus on empathy + soft CTA

## Paid TikTok Ad Strategy

### Creative Selection
- Run organic for 7 days first
- Identify top 3 by: saves, shares, comments, completion rate
- Boost those as paid ads

### Ad Format
- **Spark Ads** (boost organic posts, keeps comments/engagement)
- NOT standard TikTok ads (too obviously ads, lower trust)

### Targeting
- Women 35-65 (TikTok demo skews younger than Facebook)
- Interests: Catholic faith, prayer, Christian women, caregiving, parenting adult children
- Lookalike: Website visitors (after first 100+ conversations)

### Budget
- $20-30/day per creative
- Run for 7 days minimum before evaluating
- Scale winners aggressively (TikTok ads can scale fast)

## Success Metrics

### Organic Content
- **Completion rate >45%** (did they watch to the end?)
- **Save rate >5%** (are they keeping this for later?)
- **Share rate >3%** (are they sending to friends/mom?)
- **Comments:** Quality over quantity (are people sharing their stories?)

### Paid Content
- **CTR >2%** (click to bio link)
- **CPC <$0.75** (cost per click)
- **Conversation starts:** Track in dashboard (UTM params on bio link)

### The Real Metric
**Cost per conversation start.** If you're getting conversations for <$5, scale aggressively.

## What NOT to Do on TikTok

### Avoid
- ❌ Over-produced content (looks like an ad = scroll)
- ❌ Trending audio that doesn't match emotion (breaks authenticity)
- ❌ "Link in bio!" shouted in video (kills trust)
- ❌ Multiple CTAs (pick one: link in bio)
- ❌ Fake emotion (TikTok can smell this)
- ❌ Explaining too much (show, don't tell)

### Do
- ✅ Raw, real emotion (even if technically "imperfect")
- ✅ Subtlety (Donna appears naturally, not pushed)
- ✅ Story over sales (entertainment/value first)
- ✅ Vulnerability (people connect with honest struggle)
- ✅ Let comments build community (reply to every single one)
