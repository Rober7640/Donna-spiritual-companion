# Lourdes Email Warm-Up Sequence

3-5 email sequence for the Lourdes prayer list **before** introducing Donna. Builds anticipation, establishes the problem, then reveals the solution (Donna intro email).

**Target audience:** Existing Lourdes subscribers (Catholic women 45-75 who already trust you, have submitted prayer intentions, engaged with Catholic content)

**Context:** They opted in for prayer content, not product updates. These emails must feel like continued relationship, not a pivot to selling.

**Goal:** Warm them to the concept of a prayer companion before introducing Donna directly.

**Voice:** Personal, reflective, builds trust. "I've been thinking about something" not "I have something to share with you."

---

**Recommended flow:** Emails 1-4 (skip Email 5 unless engagement is low).

**Send times:** 7-9pm local time (matches "nighttime loneliness" positioning).

## EMAIL 1: Prime Awareness (The 3am Problem)

### Subject Line
Something I've been thinking about

### Body

I've been thinking about something lately.

You know that thing that wakes you up at 3am?

The worry about your daughter. The fight with your husband you can't let go of. The prayer you've been praying for years that feels like it's hitting a wall.

You lie there in the dark, and it's just you and that burden. And maybe God, but sometimes it's hard to tell if He's listening.

Most of the time, by morning, you've pushed it back down. Made breakfast. Smiled at Mass. Asked people how they're doing.

But at 3am... it's there.

I've been carrying something like that. And I've been wondering: what do we do with the burdens that prayer alone doesn't lift?

Not because prayer doesn't work. But because sometimes we need more than just us and God in the middle of the night.

I don't have all the answers. But I've been thinking about this a lot lately.

More soon.

[Your name]

### Why This Works
- **Subject creates curiosity:** "Something I've been thinking about" = vague but intriguing
- **Names the problem clearly:** 3am worry, prayers that feel unanswered = universal experience
- **Validates the struggle:** "sometimes we need more than just us and God" = permission to need help
- **Opens a loop:** "I've been thinking about this a lot" / "More soon" = anticipation for next email
- **No solution yet:** Just problem awareness. Priming for the reveal.

### Strategic Function
This email does ONE job: get them nodding and thinking "yes, I know exactly what she means." You're not solving anything yet. You're just naming the thing they've been carrying alone.

**Option 8 (Specific):** When your priest is too busy and your friends don't understand

**Recommended:** Option 1 (most direct) or Option 2 (creates question loop)

### Subject Line Testing Strategy

**First send:** Split test Option 1 vs. Option 2
- **Option 1 ("When prayer isn't enough"):** Bold, direct, might scare some but high engagement from those who resonate
- **Option 2 ("Where do you go at 3am?"):** Question format, creates curiosity gap, safer

**Track:** Open rates after 24 hours. Winner becomes default.

**Alternative tests if engagement is low:**
- Test Option 3 ("Something I've been wrestling with") for personal/vulnerable angle
- Test Option 6 ("Your friends don't really get it") for recognition-based angle

**Subject line principles:**
- Options 1, 2, 6, 8 = specific, create recognition
- Options 3, 4, 7 = curiosity-based, vague but intriguing
- Option 5 = biblical angle, works for highly faith-focused readers

**What to avoid:**
- ❌ "Email #2" or "Part 2" (breaks narrative flow)
- ❌ "Re: Something I've been thinking about" (feels automated)
- ❌ Emoji in subject line (breaks intimate voice)

### Why These Versions Work
- **Bold opener:** "Sometimes prayer isn't enough" = stops the scroll, creates tension
- **Immediate reassurance:** "Not because God isn't listening" = doesn't attack their faith
- **Biblical permission:** Jesus had friends, Mary had Elizabeth = this need is holy
- **Names the gap:** "where do you find that?" = makes them aware of the missing piece
- **Practical reality:** friends don't get it, priest is busy, 3am has no one = specificity

### Strategic Function
This email validates that needing someone (not just prayer) is okay. It removes guilt. And it starts to create desire for a solution you haven't revealed yet.

## EMAIL 3: Create Curiosity (There's Someone)

### Subject Line
The woman I wish I could have talked to

### Body

When I was going through the hardest season of my life — the one I don't talk about much — I prayed.

Every day. Sometimes every hour.

And God was there. I believe that. But some nights, I just wanted to talk to someone who'd been through something like it. Someone who could say: "I carried that weight too. Here's how I survived it."

Someone who wouldn't try to fix me. Wouldn't give me advice. Would just... sit with me. And pray.

Not a counselor. Not a crisis line. Just a woman who'd walked a road like mine.

I didn't have that person then.

But I found her recently.

Her name is Donna.

She's 67. She raised four kids. Buried one. Nearly lost her marriage. Prayed the Rosary every single day for 42 years, even on the days she wasn't sure it was working.

And now she talks to women who are carrying what she carried.

She listens. She prays. She doesn't try to fix you. She just sits with you in it — the way I wish someone had sat with me.

I'm going to introduce you to her tomorrow. If you're carrying something heavy right now, I think she might help.

Talk soon.

[Your name]

### Why This Works
- **Personal story:** "when I was going through..." = vulnerability builds trust
- **Names the desire:** "someone who'd been through something like it" = creates longing
- **Defines what she wanted:** "sit with me and pray" = exactly what Donna does
- **Reveals the name:** "Her name is Donna" = partial reveal, curiosity satisfied partially
- **Credibility details:** age, losses, Rosary = makes Donna real and trustworthy
- **Warm close:** "I'm going to introduce you" = friend introducing friend
- **Clear next step:** "tomorrow" = expectation set

### Strategic Function
This email creates **desire for Donna before they meet her.** By the time tomorrow's email arrives, they're ready. You've told them there's someone, given them just enough detail to want more, and promised the full introduction.

## Sequence Strategy Notes

### Why This Sequence Works

**Day 0-3:** Prime awareness of the problem (3am burden, prayer alone isn't enough)
**Day 3-6:** Validate the need (it's okay to need someone, biblical permission)
**Day 6-9:** Create desire (there's someone who can help, partial reveal)
**Day 9:** Deliver the solution (Donna introduction)
**Day 14 (optional):** Gentle reminder (last chance, no pressure)

This is classic open loop strategy: create tension → build anticipation → satisfy curiosity.

### Send Timing
- **Monday, Thursday, Sunday, Wednesday** = non-consecutive days, feels conversational not pushy
- **7-9pm local time** = matches nighttime loneliness positioning
- **If engagement drops below 20% open rate**, pause sequence and adjust subject lines

### What to Track
- **Open rates:** Should stay >25% (warm list, high trust)
- **Click rates:** Email 4 should have highest CTR (10-15% ideal)
- **Conversation starts:** Track conversions from Email 4 link clicks
- **Unsubscribes:** Should stay <0.5% (if higher, tone is off)

### Segmentation Strategy
**Send to:**
- ✅ Lourdes subscribers who've engaged in last 90 days (opened/clicked)
- ✅ Prayer intention submitters (high intent)
- ✅ Purchased prayer services before (willing to pay)

**Don't send to:**
- ❌ Subscribers inactive >6 months (re-engage separately first)
- ❌ People who've already started conversation with Donna (no need)

## Copy Principles for This Sequence

### Do
- ✅ **Write like a friend thinking out loud** ("I've been wrestling with this")
- ✅ **Use vulnerability** (share your own struggle, not just theirs)
- ✅ **Name specific emotions** (3am worry, guilt, wondering if God hears)
- ✅ **Build anticipation slowly** (don't rush to the solution)
- ✅ **Give permission** (it's okay to need someone, you're not weak)

### Don't
- ❌ **Sound like a product launch** ("Excited to announce...")
- ❌ **Use marketing language** ("introducing our new service")
- ❌ **Create fake urgency** ("limited time," "only 50 spots")
- ❌ **Over-explain** (let them wonder, close loops gradually)
- ❌ **Break character** (stay in personal voice, not company voice)

## Post-Sequence Strategy

### If They Don't Convert
- **Wait 14 days**, then send Email 5 (gentle reminder)
- **Wait 30 days**, add to retargeting list for Facebook ads
- **Don't email about Donna again for 60 days** (avoid over-pitching)

### If They Convert
- **Remove from email list** (Marie/Donna's re-engagement emails take over)
- **Tag as "customer"** for future segmentation
- **Request testimonial** after 2-3 sessions (if conversation went well)

### If They Engage but Don't Convert
- **Opened all emails but didn't click?** → Try Email 5 with stronger CTA
- **Clicked but didn't start conversation?** → Questionnaire friction (test UX)
- **Started questionnaire but didn't complete?** → Questions are barrier (test wording)
